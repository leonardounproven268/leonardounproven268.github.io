About

Background

I am an Assistant Professor in the Department of Mathematics and Statistics at California State University, Long Beach. My work centers on inverse problems — recovering unknown sources, coefficients, and initial conditions in partial differential equations from limited or noisy measurements.

A recurring theme in my research is designing globally convergent numerical methods that do not rely on a good initial guess, built on Carleman estimates, convexification, and contraction-mapping techniques. I validate these methods on both simulated and experimental data, and increasingly connect them with Bayesian optimization and machine learning.

Before joining CSULB, I was a Postdoctoral Research Scholar at North Carolina State University under mentor Hien Tran, and earned my Ph.D. in Applied Mathematics at the University of North Carolina at Charlotte under advisor Loc Nguyen.

Research

Interests

Inverse Problems Numerical Analysis Numerical Methods for PDEs Nonlinear PDEs Carleman Estimates & Convexification Bayesian Optimization Machine Learning

My research develops and analyzes numerical methods for ill-posed inverse problems arising in imaging, scattering, and heat conduction. The methods I build come with convergence guarantees and are tested against experimental measurements.

Current directions include time-dimensional reduction methods for reconstructing initial data (Maxwell and Navier–Stokes equations), phaseless inverse scattering, and expert-guided causal Bayesian optimization — bridging classical inverse-problem theory with modern learning-based approaches.
